What it is

A real research project you take on yourself, using Claude Code at every stage, with a report that documents both what you found and how you used AI to get there.

Pick a topic that matters to you
  • A piece of your dissertation or second-year paper
  • An existing pilot, dataset, or analysis you have been meaning to run
  • A mini project sparked by something we discussed in class

Three viable shapes: a dissertation pre-analysis, a replication-and-extension of a published paper, or a methodological innovation. Individual project — no teams.

The discipline
Every significant prompt and its output goes into your AI Usage Log. Document failures alongside successes — that is where the learning is.

Two milestones

Milestone 1 — Session 12 (last day)
10-minute proposal presentation + 3-minute Q&A

Present your research plan: question, methodology, AI-assisted workflow, and any preliminary results or proof-of-concept. The Q&A is the most useful feedback you will get all semester — you have a month to act on it before submitting the report.

Milestone 2 — ~1 month after Session 12
Final research report + reproducibility package

A piece of work you can build on after the course ends — a working paper, a dissertation chapter, or a methods note. Exact deadline announced by Session 10.

The final report package

Research report (~25 pages)

Standard structure — intro, lit review, methods, results, discussion — plus a dedicated AI Methodology section covering what you used, what you validated, and what you would do differently. Two to three pages is enough.

Reproducibility package (ZIP)

Code, data (or detailed access instructions), CLAUDE.md, key prompts with annotations, and a top-level README. Benchmark: a smart PhD student in another department should be able to retrace your workflow six months from now.

AI Usage Log (CSV or markdown table)

Date · stage · tool · prompt summary · output quality · validation step. Honesty over polish. Include failures.

Three non-negotiables
  • Verify every citation. Hallucinated references cost points and trust.
  • Document failures. A log showing only successes signals weak engagement.
  • Your own judgment. AI augments — the argument and conclusions are yours.
